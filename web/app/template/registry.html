Registry

It is common in Chinese culture for wedding guests to give a monetary gift to the bride and groom. Traditionally, the gift is provided in a red envelope called hóngbāo (红包) or lai see (利是). We would be honored to receive a red envelope in lieu of a registry gift, and will have a box at reception for the envelopes and cards.

Red Envelopes

The tradition of giving red envelopes in Chinese culture is a monetary gift and a gesture of appreciation during holidays or special occasions such as weddings, graduations, or the birth of a baby.

The red color of the envelope symbolizes good luck and is a symbol to ward off evil spirits. The amount you choose for the red envelope is very personal and subjective. However, the amount given in red envelopes should not include the number '4'—that means no $4, $40, or $400 amounts—as the pronunciation of 'four' in Chinese sounds like the word for death. Amounts including the number '8' will bring good luck and prosperity.
